Guillermo González Camarena

El ingeniero - inventor mexicano más reconocido en el país

Biography (childhood and youth) Ing. Guillermo González

  • February 17, 1917 Born in Guadalajara, Jalisco
  • 1919 After living in Guadalajara, his family moved to Mexico City when Guillermo was two years old.
  • 1925 While still a child he made toys powered by electricity, at the age of eight he managed to make his first radio transmitter
  • 1929 He built his first amateur radio
  • 1938 He invented the "Chromoscopic Adapter for Television Sets", the first color transmission system for television, which was patented on August 19, 1940.
  • 1939 He graduated from the Higher School of Mechanical and Electrical Engineering of the National Polytechnic Institute (IPN)
  • 1941 He obtained his first radio license two years later.
  • August 10, 1942 Filed the patent application in the United States of America. From this first system, different more elaborate procedures began to emerge in various countries, but all based on his original idea. In addition, the inventor filed improvements to his patent for color television systems in 1958.
  • August 31, 1946 González Camarena sent the first color transmission from his laboratory at the offices of La Liga Mexicana de Radio Experimentos, at Calle de Lucerna No. 1, in Mexico City. The video signal was transmitted on the 115 MHz frequency and on the 40-meter audio band.
  • 1948 He founded the Gon-Cam Laboratories, where they began to work, spontaneously, with other radio experimenters.
  • April 18, 1965 He died when he was returning from inspecting the repeater transmitter of Channel 5 on the hill of Las Lajas, Veracruz, to extend the signal of the television network generated in Mexico City to that eastern region of the country, he found death at the age of 48. age in a car accident in the municipality of Amozoc. The news of the unfortunate event was broadcast on radio and television, as well as the funeral events. As a sign of mourning, television broadcasts were interrupted throughout the day.

"González Camarena was a versatile character who found great taste in music and folklore. He was a composer of some beautiful songs. The best known bears the name of “Río colorado”. About this piece it is said that thanks to its success —and the royalties that Camarena received for it— it was possible to finance the creation of the sequential field trichromatic system. He was also an amateur astronomer and a great reader of Mexican history."
